# HomeWise — Product Overview

## What It Is
HomeWise is a practical home and workspace improvement advisor. It helps everyday people make meaningful upgrades to their living and working spaces — focused on ergonomics, interior organization, lighting, and daily comfort. No interior designer required. No big budget. Just clear, actionable advice you can act on this weekend.

## Users
- **Office workers** *(primary)* — people sitting at desks all day, accumulating physical strain they haven't recognized as a problem; the core audience
- **Field workers & physical laborers** — construction workers, healthcare staff, warehouse workers, delivery drivers; people who bend, lift, and strain their bodies through entire shifts without realizing the cumulative damage
- **Remote workers & students** — people spending long hours at a home desk who want a healthier, more productive setup
- **Homeowners & renters** — anyone looking to improve the comfort, function, and physical safety of their space
- **Practically-minded improvers** — people who sense something is off but have never had anyone explain what, or how to fix it

**Core audience insight**: Most people are silently harming themselves every day — not out of carelessness, but because no one ever told them otherwise. This applies equally to someone hunched at a monitor and someone bending over a workbench for eight hours. HomeWise is the first voice that does.

## Positioning
HomeWise sits between "just Google it" and "hire a professional." It's the practical middle path: structured, personalized, and immediately actionable — the knowledgeable friend who's good at this stuff, not a consultant.

The core insight HomeWise is built on: most people are silently harming their bodies at work every day — not because they're careless, but because no one ever told them what good posture or a well-set-up space actually looks like. HomeWise is the first voice that does. Plainly. Without jargon or judgment.

Where Pinterest and Houzz lean toward aesthetics and aspiration, HomeWise leans toward function and action: specific things you can do today, in your actual space, with your actual budget.

## Core Features
1. **Space Assessment** — A short questionnaire that surfaces the top improvement opportunities for your room or setup
2. **Ergonomics Checker** — Guided analysis of desk, chair, monitor, keyboard, and lighting with concrete fix-it recommendations
3. **Room Tips Browser** — Actionable tips organized by room type: home office, bedroom, kitchen, living room
4. **Improvement Tracker** — A personal checklist to mark off improvements as you complete them, with a sense of visible progress
5. **Quick Wins Library** — Curated high-impact, low-cost changes ranked by effort and payoff — many costing nothing at all

## Brand & Tone
- **Tone**: Practical, direct, encouraging — no jargon, no fluff, no condescension
- **Voice**: The knowledgeable friend who's genuinely good at home stuff; unpretentious and clear
- **Visual identity**: Clean, calm, minimal — warm sage teal palette, airy layouts, functional over decorative
- **Primary color**: `#4a9e8a` (sage teal) — calm, practical, approachable
- **Secondary / dark**: `#1a2e2b` (deep teal) — grounding, premium
- **Accent**: `#f0a04b` (warm amber) — highlights, numbered callouts

## Scope (MVP)
Web application covering:
- Space Assessment questionnaire
- Ergonomics Checker (home office / desk setup focus)
- Room Tips Browser (4 room types: home office, bedroom, kitchen, living room)
- Improvement Tracker (personal progress checklist)
- Quick Wins Library (ranked, filterable)

Post-MVP: Photo analysis of your actual space, shareable improvement plans, community-submitted tips, mobile app.

## Strategic Principles
- **Practical over pretty** — every feature must produce an action, not just inspiration
- **Low friction** — users should get their first recommendation in under 5 minutes
- **No expertise required** — all advice uses plain language; no design or ergonomics background assumed
- **Accessible to all budgets** — tips span free (rearrange your furniture) to low-cost (a $15 monitor riser)
